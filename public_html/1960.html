1950-1960s

Circuit Switching Problem

Designed in 1878, circuit-switching was a method of creating a communications network between two people. Telephone calls were connected by two network nodes and established a channel. If the connection between two telephones is broken, the call is lost. This weakness became a national threat to the military when they were afraid of a Soviet Nuclear Attack. All the telephone lines were centralized in one location, and if an enemy blew up that location, no one would be able to communicate. USA knew they needed an indestructible communications network.

General Dwight Eisenhower

General Dwight Eisenhower, supreme commander in World War II, and the 34th president of USA, played a big role in technology research. He saw that the future of war is technology. In other words, it will be very technologically advanced stuff that makes USA win economically. Thus, Eisenhower decided to invest in research, creating DARPA.

DARPA

DARPA, founded in 1958, was a government agency created for advanced research projects. Their main goal was to solve the circuit-switching problem. Soon, DARPA became a centralized agency that did all of the research for universities, big companies and the military. This provided some degree of civilian control and oversight through the congress of US.

J.C.R Licklider

J.C.R Licklider, was an American psychologist and computer scientist who is considered to be one of the notable figures in the development of the internet. Licklider created the idea of a universal network. In other words, he invented a way that a message could self-check itself that it was correct without sending a complete copy of the message again. His work invented a new field called information theory and his mathematics laid the theoretical groundwork for what a network would be.

Packet-Switching

Luckily, the solution to the circuit switching problem was packet-switching. Packet-switching is a new approach to data transmission, where a message is divided into several packets that can be routed independently to their destination. Information flows through packets to find its way to the network. Each packet has an address of where it wants to go and you launch that packet into the network and it follows a path to the address. A node would decide where to send each packet, its like a post office sending out postcards. But a node will keep a copy of the packets and keep sending them out until they arrive successfully at the next node. Circuit switching doesn’t work well because if one wire is cut, there is no way to automatically send a signal again, and no way to check if information is correct. With data, you can’t see what’s going through the wire, you need analytics to self-correct messages on the network. Licklider proposed this to ARPA and they combined his theory with electrical engineers and created the universal network.
