1970-1980s

[Image. From Pixabay by xresch. Jan 19,2018. (https://pixabay.com/images/id-3088958/)]

Arpanet

ARPANET became the foundation for the internet by using the packet-switching technology. It made it easy for people to connect with each other and effectively communicate. 37 computers connected over USA to create the backbone of ARPAnet, comprised of UCLA, MIT, Stanford research institute , etc..

[Image. From Pixabay by sumanly. Mar 15 ,2018. (https://pixabay.com/images/id-3228704/)]

Vincent Cerf and Robert Kahn

More and more researchers focused on investigating new protocols capable of interconnecting networks. Vincent Cerf and Robert Kahn, who met in UCLA, collaborated on the design of software called the Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol (TCP/IP). Together, they created the architecture of the internet and are known as “Fathers of the Internet”. They focused on finding a way to get all computers to talk to each other and figured out a way to get networks to send packets back and forth.

[Image. From Pixabay by designwebjae. Jan 26 ,2018. (https://pixabay.com/images/id-3109378/)]

IP and DNS

IP introduced the basic system of numerical addresses. An address such as 129.89.43.5 identifies the computer to which a data packet should be sent to and the network on which it can be found. However, ARPAnet users were used to specifying computers by name or email addresses. Thus, a new domain name system (DNS) and TCP/IP protocols replaced the old ARPAnet- connected computers. In the DNS, high-level domains had functional codes such as .gov, .edu, .mil, or national codes such as .uk, .usa. Organizations received domain names ending in .edu, or .com. These domain names covered the assigned numerical IP addresses associated with individual computers.
